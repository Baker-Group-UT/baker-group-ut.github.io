Maxwell Poster is a 5th year Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at The University of Texas at Austin. His research focuses on quantum error correction and fault-tolerant quantum architectures, with an emphasis on heterogeneous noise models and noise-adaptive mapping and scheduling. His current work explores in-situ recalibration for surface-code QEC, logical mappings and factory architectures for qLDPC codes, and concatenation/code-deformation techniques under biased noise. He has also published work on cyclic qubit mappings for drift and leakage mitigation in near-term quantum architectures.

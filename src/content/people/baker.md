Jonathan Baker is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical & Computer
Engineering at UT Austin, where he leads the group's work on the architecture
of error-corrected quantum computers. Before UT, he was a research scientist at
the Duke Quantum Center and completed his PhD in computer science at the
University of Chicago, advised by Fred Chong.

The group studies the layers between abstract fault-tolerant protocols and
real machines: how to compile programs onto lattice surgery schedules, how to
decode syndromes under realistic latency budgets, and how to make principled
trade-offs across the control stack.

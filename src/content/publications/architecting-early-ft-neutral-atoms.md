A full-stack architectural study of neutral-atom systems in the early fault-tolerant
regime, characterizing the trade-offs between atom-array connectivity, logical-qubit
overheads, and the program classes that can realistically beat classical baselines
at near-term scales. The paper maps out where the "quantum advantage" line currently
sits for this platform and identifies the architectural levers that move it.
